### Biography

I'm a chemical engineer from Mumbai, India. I was fortunate enough to receive my
bachelor's degree in chemical engineering from the Institute of Chemical Technology,
Mumbai (ICT, formerly UDCT). A summer internship at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT)
Bombay in Prof. Ratul Dasgupta's lab---wherein I performed computational fluid dynamics
simulations for free surface flows---ignited my interest in the use of computer simulations
for research. After graduating from ICT, I moved to the US to pursue
a doctorate in chemical engineering at Purdue University in West Lafayette, Indiana.
Here, in Prof. Jeffrey Greeley's computational catalysis group, I study how multimetallic
alloys catalyze electrochemical and thermal reactions using density functional theory (DFT)
calculations and deep learning. My professional goal remains the application of computational modelling
and applied mathematics to solve pressing fundamental problems in chemical engineering and
allied fields.
{: .justify}

Additionally, it is my life's mission to communicate scientific concepts
to curious minds in a manner that keeps their enthusiasm alive. To that end, I maintain a
[personal blog](https://medium.com/@ChemAndCode) on Medium geared towards introducing
practically relevant concepts from chemistry, physics, engineering, and mathematics.
I believe that you only understand something well when you can compute it, so programming is my
preferred method of teaching. My articles have received more than 100,000 views till date, and
many positive testimonials from both students and educators, for which I am eternally grateful.
{: .justify}
